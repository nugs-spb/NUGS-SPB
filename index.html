NUGS Saint Petersburg/Ghana Community

Rebuilding an all inclusive Vibrant Union

Knowing how quick the number of Ghanaians was incredibly increasing in Saint Petersburg for the purposes of education and work, there was the need to bring all of us( Ghanaians) under one umbrella; the Ghana Community Saint Petersburg. Although around same time the student body was much organized and had formed NUGS Petersburg( in the year 2008) after the establishment of NUGS Moscow, the community became a merger between the students and workers living in Saint Petersburg. The purpose of the union was to foster great unity amongst all Ghanaians living in Saint Petersburg and to provide many opportunities ranging from part time jobs, summer jobs and resolution of document issues. Undoubtedly, the union since then has become the mouth piece of all Ghanains in Petersburg and offers great link between the Ghana Embassy, Moscow and the Ghanaian populace in Petersburg, Russia.
Daniel K. Oppong.
President Nugs Spb

sponsorship, summer jobs, summer camps, football competitions, educational seminars, annual get together.
NUGS SPB is a must join student union.

The financial status of every union has always been the backbone of every union without which a union can barely stand. To run the Union, Nugs Petersburg are tasked to make monthly contributions. These monthly monies are apportioned according to set down rules of the union where respective percentages of the total revenue collected go into :

  • Welfare
  • Football team
  • Visiting and media team
  • Annual Get-together
  • Graduation/Child naming and other ceremonies
  • Summer camps and Picnics

Cynthia Dedaa Danso

Specialist-Pharmacy honours
St.Petersburg State Chemical and Pharmaceutical academy

Russia is the largest and one of the most beautiful countries in the world. St. Petersburg is located on the Finnish Gulf and is also referred to as the cultural capital. The city boasts of historic architecture, numerous islands, rivers and canals. Studying here has been a hurdle race of sorts, as there were many challenges to overcome; the language barrier, local cuisine, extreme erratic weather conditions, different social psychologies and a different educational system from that of most countries. It was quite difficult to deal with these challenges in the beginning but through it all I learnt things will get better just as summer always shows up despite the depressingly long and unforgiving Russian winter. My advice to anyone embarking on the Russian educational journey is firstly to have an open mind, to focus and be determined to reach the finish line and to keep a positive attitude.
